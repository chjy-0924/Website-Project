Our Study Habits:

By: Cherry Joy Toribio

I use various study habits to enhance my academic performance and improve my retention and understanding. However, one study habit or technique I commonly use is the Pomodoro Technique. The Pomodoro Technique helps balance work and break time and is a time management method designed to help you focus on specific tasks. It involves working in focused intervals of 25 minutes, called "Pomodoros," followed by a 5-minute break. After completing four Pomodoros, I take a longer break of 15 to 30 minutes. This technique helps me improve focus, avoid burnout, and boost productivity. Aside from that technique, I also enjoy studying with friends, such as testing one another and using active recall. With active recall, I try to retrieve information from memory without looking at my notes, then review my notes to identify and fill in the gaps. Lastly, I never forget my daily dose of coffee, which helps boost my alertness, elevate my mood, and increase my energy.

By: Raven Nolasco

My study habits are structured and effective, helping me stay focused and productive. I prefer to study with instrumental background music to ease my mind and create a calming atmosphere. I set a schedule for my tasks to ensure that I stay organized and on track. While reviewing, I take detailed notes and use flashcards to reinforce my understanding of the material. I also prioritize my well-being by taking 15-30 minute breaks to refresh my mind and eating snacks occasionally to maintain my energy levels. Additionally, I keep my to-do tasks organized, which helps me manage my time efficiently and reduce stress. These habits contribute to a balanced and efficient study routine.

By: Dwayne Fajardo

I keep my study habits organized and structured so my time is used most effectively. I allocate specific time slots for studying each day, focusing on maintaining consistency. I prioritize understanding concepts thoroughly rather than just memorizing information. Taking regular breaks helps me stay focused and retain information better. Additionally, I make use of tools like notes, flashcards, and practice tests to reinforce my learning effectively.
